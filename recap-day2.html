Legal Team Collaboration & Contracting Best Practices

Aaron
Aaron explained the legal department's role in supporting sales, outlined best practices for engaging legal early, and detailed strategies for efficient contract negotiation and leveraging contracting vehicles.
  • Legal's Role & Teamwork

    Aaron clarified that legal's primary goal is building company value and supporting long-term customer relationships — not just protecting the company. Sales and legal need to work closely together throughout the deal process, not just at the end.

  • Contracting Strategies

    Start with pre-negotiated contracting vehicles (NASPO, GSA) or Accela's standard paper to minimize friction. This approach saves significant time and resources compared to negotiating on customer paper every time.

  • Negotiation Process

    Aaron described the "go slow to go fast" drafting approach, the two-round negotiation method, and the four legal pillars: liability, indemnification, warranties, and IP. Live conversations resolve issues far more efficiently than email threads.

  • Partner & Self-Service Tools

    Involve partners early for contracting vehicles. Upcoming AI-powered tools will generate standard legal documents and empower sales to handle routine contract modifications without waiting on legal.

Managed Services: Sizing, KPIs & Governance

Cary Phillips, Jonathan Ward, Eric McCauley, Brenda DeGregory
Cary led a deep dive on managed services sizing, KPI alignment, data visibility, and how to structure and differentiate service packages for different customer needs.
  • Labor Reduction & Proactive Support

    The program takes labor-intensive tasks off the customer's plate, proactively addressing production issues and incomplete testing cycles. Small, high-impact changes are identified through direct user feedback and workflow analysis.

  • Business Insights & Automated Testing

    Business insights and automated testing are positioned as creative solutions for customers who may not want traditional incident or enhancement support. These services help farm accounts and identify upsell opportunities.

  • KPI Alignment & Data Visibility

    Jonathan explained how managed services provides ongoing analytics and scorecards to help agencies track performance. Agencies often lack visibility into their own processes and rely on manual reporting — the program fills that gap with automated data analysis.

  • Package Customization & Tiers

    Packages come in three tiers: entry-level core (a "taste" to encourage upsell), middle on-demand with hours buckets, and enterprise all-inclusive. The embedded team option is the "Cadillac" for customers needing dedicated resources, as done with California DCC.

  • Onshore Resourcing & Margins

    Clarify onshore resourcing requirements early — these significantly affect project margins. Ask about this during the sales process to ensure accurate contract stipulations and avoid margin surprises post-close.

  • Governance vs. Business Insights

    Jonathan's team handles enterprise-level KPI analysis across the platform, while managed services focuses on detailed, process-specific analysis (licensing, permitting). The two approaches complement each other — enterprise analysis identifies areas that managed services can then optimize in detail.

Novotx V6 Positioning & Demonstration

Animesh Irkulla, Reid Christianson
Animesh and Reid led a comprehensive walkthrough of the Novotx V6 platform, highlighting its differentiators and demonstrating how these features address challenges faced by public agencies in infrastructure management.
  • Industry Challenges Overview

    Animesh and Reid described the current state of public infrastructure — water loss, aging assets, poor road conditions — and how agencies struggle with reactive maintenance, paper-based processes, and siloed data. Novotx V6 addresses all of these head-on.

  • Platform Differentiators

    Five key differentiators: advanced GIS integration, robust capital planning, automation capabilities, enterprise scalability, and AI-driven features. Each addresses specific pain points in asset management that competitors like Trimble CityWorks don't solve as comprehensively.

  • GIS Integration

    Reid demonstrated how Novotx supports various asset types, tracks asset condition and work orders spatially, and enables advanced features like 3D GIS for field crews — positioning it as a leader in spatial asset management.

  • Capital Planning

    The capital planning module allows agencies to model asset deterioration, plan maintenance and repairs, and justify budget requests with data-driven projections. Subject matter expert Cody Steggle contributed to its development.

  • Automation & Connected Operations

    No-code automation for work orders includes cross-departmental workflows, supervisor sign-offs, and automated GIS updates. These features reduce manual effort and improve data quality compared to competitors.

  • AI & Integration Features

    AI capabilities include voice-to-fill inspection forms and flexible integration with various AI models, plus out-of-the-box integrations with platforms like Accela. Agencies can adapt AI to their workflows while maintaining security and governance.

  • Customer Enablement Resources

    Resources in development include a cloud agent for competitive intelligence, RFP templates, and Figma-style presentation tools. The team requested feedback to further tailor these assets to sales needs.

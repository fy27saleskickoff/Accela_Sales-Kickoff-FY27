From Stuck to a $425K Expansion

Jonathan Ward & Matt Donovan

Session Details

How Sales + Government Strategy & Innovation turned executive pressure into a win. Milwaukee’s Department of Neighborhood Services runs Accela across 6 modules, 208 record types, and ~607K records — a mission-critical platform spanning permitting, enforcement, and licensing. The Mayor’s office made permitting speed a priority, but after five years fighting a failed e-PlanSoft implementation, the city was stuck with no clear place to start. A strategic assessment gave the account focus — turning pressure into a decision-grade roadmap tied to the Mayor’s priorities. Sales opened the door, Strategy & Innovation earned the trust in the middle, and Sales sealed it — a repeatable play for the field.

Getting There
Travel To Charleston
Everything you need to know about getting to the Francis Marion Hotel.

Charleston International Airport (CHS)

The airport is about 12 miles from the hotel. Plan for a 20–25 minute ride depending on traffic. All major airlines serve CHS with direct flights from most US hubs.

12 miles • ~20 min to hotel
Rideshare Pickup Info →

Getting To The Hotel

Uber / Lyft — Easiest option. $20–25 for a standard ride. Pickup is at designated rideshare zones across the roadway from baggage claim.

Taxi — Available curbside at the ground transportation booth. Approximately $30–35 metered fare.

Francis Marion Hotel →

Parking

Self-park — Adjacent city garage. $1 per 20 min, max $24/day.

Valet — $40 + tax per night. Unlimited in/out. Pull into valet lane on King Street.

Directions & Map →

July Weather

Charleston in late July is hot and humid. Expect highs around 90°F with high humidity. Afternoon thunderstorms are common but usually pass quickly.

Highs ~90°F • Humid • Pack light

Dining Tips

Most restaurants on King Street are within a 5–10 minute walk from the hotel. For team dinners on Wednesday night, coordinate with your VP on reservations. Ask the hotel concierge for last-minute availability. Keep it casual — the best food in Charleston does not require a jacket.

Sightseeing
Discover Charleston
The Holy City has no shortage of incredible food, history, and things to do. Most of these are walking distance from the hotel.
15 min walk

Rainbow Row

83-107 East Bay Street

Thirteen pastel-colored historic houses dating to the 1740s. The most photographed spot in Charleston. Best in early morning light.

12 min walk

Waterfront Park

Vendue Range at Concord Street

Beautiful harbor views, swinging benches, and the iconic Pineapple Fountain. Perfect for a morning walk or evening stroll.

20 min walk

The Battery

South tip of the peninsula

Seawall promenade with antebellum mansions, Civil War cannons, live oaks, and the best sunset views in the city.

You are here

King Street

Right outside the hotel

Charleston’s main shopping and dining corridor. Boutiques, galleries, restaurants, and bars for blocks in every direction.

7 min walk

City Market

188 Meeting Street

One of the oldest public markets in America. Four blocks of local vendors, Gullah sweetgrass baskets, and souvenirs.

15 min walk

International African American Museum

14 Wharfside Street

On the site of Gadsden’s Wharf. A powerful, essential experience. Allow 2–3 hours.

Beaches And Day Trips

If you are extending your trip or have a free afternoon.

30 min drive

Folly Beach

The most popular beach near Charleston. Surf town vibes, beachfront bars, and the Morris Island Lighthouse. Great for a half-day trip.

25 min drive

Sullivan’s Island

Quieter, more residential beach. Beautiful dunes, fewer crowds, and Poe’s Tavern. Locals’ favorite.

35 min drive

Isle Of Palms

Wide sandy beaches, family-friendly, and home to Wild Dunes Resort. Great if extending your trip through the weekend.

Walking distance

Carriage Tour

Classic Charleston experience. One-hour guided tour through the historic district. Multiple companies depart from City Market.

Walking distance

Ghost Tours

Charleston is one of the most haunted cities in America. Evening walking tours through graveyards and haunted buildings. Fun after dinner.

25 min drive

Angel Oak Tree

On Johns Island, estimated 400–500 years old. One of the most photographed trees in the world. Free to visit.

Bringing Family?
Family-Friendly Charleston
If your family is joining you in Charleston, there is plenty to keep everyone entertained while you’re in sessions.

South Carolina Aquarium

100 Aquarium Wharf (15 min walk)

Sea turtles, touch tanks, river otters, and local marine life. Great for kids of all ages. Allow 2–3 hours.

Patriots Point

40 Patriots Point Road, Mt. Pleasant (15 min drive)

Home to the USS Yorktown aircraft carrier. Explore the flight deck, below-deck compartments, and the Medal of Honor Museum.

Children’s Museum Of The Lowcountry

25 Ann Street (8 min walk)

Interactive exhibits for kids ages 0–10. Water play, building areas, art studio. Perfect for younger children.

Folly Beach

30 min drive from hotel

Sandy beaches, calm waves for kids, and a fun beach town. Rent chairs and umbrellas. Ice cream shops and casual dining nearby.

Magnolia Plantation & Gardens

3550 Ashley River Road (25 min drive)

Beautiful gardens, a petting zoo, nature train ride, and swamp boat tour. One of the oldest plantations in the US.

Waterfront Park Splash Pad

Vendue Range at Concord Street (12 min walk)

The Pineapple Fountain area doubles as a splash pad for kids in the summer heat. Free, open, and right on the harbor.

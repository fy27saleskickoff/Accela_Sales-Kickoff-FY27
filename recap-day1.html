AI Transformation in Enterprise Sales

Noam, Matt Donovan, Eric McCauley, Mike Shop, Clint Knight
  • Shift to Strategic Advisory

    The biggest change in sales is the move from relationship builder to strategic advisor. Buyers now expect deep understanding of staffing, budget pressures, and mission goals. This is what builds executive trust.

  • Outcome-Focused Selling

    Top reps focus on delivering measurable results — faster permit approvals, improved citizen experiences — rather than selling software features. The shift is from solution selling to outcome architecture.

  • Intelligence Over Information

    AI has leveled the playing field by making information widely available. The best reps now arrive with intelligence — understanding customer priorities and objections before meetings even start.

  • Quantifying Value

    Elite reps differentiate by quantifying the impact of delays, lost productivity, and revenue. The conversation shifts from price to visible value and outcomes.

  • Ecosystem Optimization

    CIOs now evaluate technology based on integration and ecosystem fit. Successful reps help optimize across departments rather than focusing on a single product.

OpenCounter AI Digital Assistant

Linh Vuong
  • AI Implementation Results

    75% of users choose AI over traditional flows. 80% completion rate among early adopters. Enhances the permit guide process by collecting richer applicant context through conversational input.

  • Safety & Security

    No hallucinations — relies on agency-configured rules. Staff can review and adjust guides. Passed third-party penetration testing with zero findings.

  • Adoption Challenges

    Cincinnati adopted successfully. Orlando and Detroit paused due to policy and leadership changes. Executive buy-in and clear AI policies are critical for sustained adoption.

  • Technical Details

    Uses prompt engineering with Anthropic models. Does not train on agency data. Can be implemented in ~2 hours if the rules engine is already configured.

  • Future Roadmap

    Permit Guide Essentials (down-market), replacement of Velocimo integration, fee calculators, and deeper ePermitHub integration for plan review requirements.

Secure Pay Product Launch

Linh Vuong, Tammy Cohen
  • Product Overview

    Fully integrated payment solution within Accela — no third-party redirects. Centralized transaction reconciliation hub. PCI Level 1 compliant payment facilitator model.

  • Early Adopter Program

    20 early adopter slots available. Four-week white glove onboarding with Payrix configuration. Priority for agencies previously using Velocimo.

  • Requirements

    Agencies must switch to WorldPay as merchant of record. Sub-merchant agreement required. PayPal not supported with Secure Pay.

  • Roadmap

    GA planned for fall. Upcoming: back office shopping cart, multi-merchant IDs, vanity URLs, self-service onboarding, refunds, and device support.

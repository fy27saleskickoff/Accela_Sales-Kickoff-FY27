AI Transformation in Enterprise Sales

Noam Reininger, Matt Donovan, Eric McCauley, Mike Schwab, Clint Knittle
Leadership discussed the profound impact of AI on enterprise sales, emphasizing the shift from relationship-based selling to strategic advisory roles, outcome-driven conversations, and ecosystem optimization.
  • Shift to Strategic Advisory

    Matt Donovan explained that the biggest change in sales is the move from relationship builder to strategic advisor. Buyers now expect a deep understanding of staffing, budget pressures, and mission goals — this is what builds executive trust, not golf outings and dinners.

  • Outcome-Focused Selling

    Tom and Cary highlighted the transition from solution selling to outcome architecture. Top reps focus on delivering measurable results — faster permit approvals, improved citizen experiences, reduced processing times — rather than listing software features. The conversation has to center on what the agency gets, not what the platform does.

  • Intelligence Over Information

    Mike Schwab noted that AI has leveled the playing field by making information widely available. The best reps now arrive with intelligence — understanding customer priorities, internal politics, and likely objections before meetings — which elevates the quality of every conversation.

  • Quantifying Value

    Eric McCauley described how elite reps differentiate themselves by quantifying the impact of delays, lost productivity, and revenue leakage. When you shift the conversation from price to visible value and measurable outcomes, budget objections shrink and urgency increases.

  • Ecosystem Optimization

    Clint Knittle discussed how CIOs now evaluate technology based on integration and ecosystem fit, not standalone features. Successful reps help customers optimize across departments rather than selling into a single use case — the broader the footprint, the stickier the deal.

Accela Product Roadmap & Platform Strategy

Noam Reininger
Noam provided an in-depth overview of Accela's product roadmap, acquisition strategy, and platform enhancements, including AI capabilities, new product launches, and the focus on reducing total cost of ownership.
  • Acquisition & Integration Strategy

    Noam detailed the rationale behind acquiring OpenCounter, ePermitHub, Novotx, and Savera AI — each acquisition strengthens the end-to-end platform and accelerates implementation for customers. The goal is a fully integrated suite, not a collection of bolted-on products.

  • AI-Enabled Product Launches

    OpenCounter AI is rolling out now. ePermitHub Auto Plan Review enters pilot in August with GA in December. AI 311 is in active development. Savera AI will transition from professional services tooling to product integration after safety guardrails are established and validated.

  • Reducing Total Cost of Ownership

    Noam emphasized the strategic goal of lowering TCO by leveraging AI for automatic configuration and real-time adaptation to new regulations. This makes the platform more accessible and cost-effective for agencies — a direct response to budget-constrained government buyers.

  • Competitive Landscape

    Against OpenGov, Tyler, and Clarity — Accela's differentiation is specialized government workflows, full platform ownership, and outcomes-driven expertise. Competitors are expanding horizontally into government; Accela goes deeper into the workflows that matter most.

  • Platform Stability & Team Changes

    After reviewing platform outages and architectural issues, Noam described the decision to bring in Jim White and consolidate product and technology leadership under Sam Ada. This is about execution discipline — fewer outages, faster releases, more predictable delivery.

OpenCounter AI Digital Assistant

Linh Vuong
Linh provided a comprehensive overview of the OpenCounter AI Digital Assistant — its technical underpinnings, early adopter results, agency concerns, and strategies for broader adoption.
  • AI Implementation & User Experience

    OpenCounter AI enhances the permit guide process by collecting richer applicant context through conversational input. 75% of users choose AI over traditional flows, with 80% completion rates among early adopters. The AI doesn't replace the permit guide — it makes it smarter and more personalized.

  • Safety, Security & Human Oversight

    The AI avoids hallucinations by relying strictly on agency-configured rules — it doesn't make things up. Staff can review and adjust AI-generated guides at any time. The system passed third-party penetration testing with zero findings. Uses prompt engineering with Anthropic models and does not train on agency data.

  • Agency Adoption Challenges

    Cincinnati successfully adopted the AI assistant and is seeing strong results. Orlando and Detroit paused deployments due to internal policy and leadership changes — not product issues. Key learning: executive buy-in and clear organizational AI policies are prerequisites for sustained adoption.

  • Implementation Speed

    If the agency's rules engine is already configured, OpenCounter AI can be implemented in approximately two hours. Staff complete a short training course before going live. The barrier is organizational readiness, not technical complexity.

  • Future Roadmap

    Upcoming: Permit Guide Essentials (down-market offering), replacement of Velocimo integration, fee calculators, and deeper integration with ePermitHub for plan review requirements. The vision is AI-assisted from first inquiry through plan review.

Secure Pay Product Launch & Onboarding

Linh Vuong, Tammy Cohen
Linh and Tammy introduced Secure Pay — its integration into the Accela platform, onboarding process, technical requirements, early adopter program, and financial impact for agencies and Accela.
  • Product Overview

    Secure Pay is a fully integrated payment solution within Accela, eliminating third-party redirects and providing a centralized hub for transaction reconciliation. End-to-end experience from the applicant's perspective — no leaving the Accela portal to pay. PCI Level 1 compliant using a payment facilitator model.

  • Early Adopter Program

    20 early adopter slots available. Four-week white glove onboarding service with Payrix configuration. Priority goes to agencies previously using Velocimo. Reps should identify eligible customers and have them complete the eligibility survey.

  • Technical & Contractual Requirements

    Agencies must switch to WorldPay as the merchant of record and sign a sub-merchant agreement. PayPal is not supported with Secure Pay. These are non-negotiable requirements — set expectations with customers early in the conversation.

  • Financial Impact & Revenue Calculation

    A sample calculation for Fresno demonstrated how Secure Pay generates revenue for Accela based on transaction volume. Guidance was provided on evaluating financial viability for specific accounts — not every agency will pencil out, so use the calculator to qualify.

  • Roadmap & GA Timeline

    General availability planned for fall. Upcoming features: back office shopping cart, multi-merchant IDs, vanity URLs, self-service onboarding, and expanded financial capabilities including refunds and device support.

AI Transformation in Enterprise Sales
Noam Reininger, Matt Donovan, Eric McCauley, Mike Schwab, Clint Knittle
Leadership discussed the profound impact of AI on enterprise sales, emphasizing the shift from relationship-based selling to strategic advisory roles, outcome-driven conversations, and ecosystem optimization.
  • Shift to Strategic Advisory

    Matt Donovan explained that the biggest change in sales is the move from relationship builder to strategic advisor. Buyers now expect a deep understanding of staffing, budget pressures, and mission goals — this is what builds executive trust.

  • Outcome-Focused Selling

    Tom and Sarah highlighted the transition from solution selling to outcome architecture. Top reps focus on delivering measurable results — faster permit approvals, improved citizen experiences — rather than listing software features.

  • Intelligence Over Information

    Mike Schwab noted that AI has leveled the playing field by making information widely available. The best reps now arrive with intelligence — understanding customer priorities and likely objections before meetings.

  • Quantifying Value

    Eric McCauley described how elite reps differentiate by quantifying the impact of delays, lost productivity, and revenue leakage — shifting the conversation from price to visible value.

  • Ecosystem Optimization

    Clint Knittle discussed how CIOs now evaluate technology based on integration and ecosystem fit. Successful reps help optimize across departments rather than selling into a single use case.

Product: OpenCounter Digital Assistant

Linh Vuong
Linh provided a comprehensive overview of the OpenCounter AI Digital Assistant — its technical underpinnings, early adopter results, agency concerns, and strategies for broader adoption.
  • AI Implementation & User Experience

    OpenCounter AI enhances the permit guide process by collecting richer applicant context through conversational input. 75% of users choose AI over traditional flows, with 80% completion rates among early adopters.

  • Safety, Security & Human Oversight

    The AI avoids hallucinations by relying strictly on agency-configured rules. Staff can review and adjust guides at any time. Passed third-party pen testing with zero findings. Uses Anthropic models and does not train on agency data.

  • Agency Adoption Challenges

    Cincinnati successfully adopted the AI assistant. Orlando and Detroit paused due to internal policy and leadership changes — not product issues. Executive buy-in and clear AI policies are prerequisites for sustained adoption.

  • Implementation Speed

    If the agency's rules engine is already configured, OpenCounter AI can be implemented in approximately two hours. Staff complete a short training course before going live.

  • Future Roadmap

    Upcoming: Permit Guide Essentials (down-market offering), replacement of Velocimo integration, fee calculators, and deeper integration with ePermitHub for plan review requirements.

Product: SecurePay + Obeya Room

Linh Vuong, Tammy Cohen
Linh and Tammy introduced Secure Pay — its integration into the Accela platform, onboarding process, technical requirements, early adopter program, and financial impact.
  • Product Overview

    Secure Pay is a fully integrated payment solution within Accela, eliminating third-party redirects and providing centralized transaction reconciliation. PCI Level 1 compliant using a payment facilitator model.

  • Early Adopter Program

    20 early adopter slots available with four-week white glove onboarding. Priority goes to agencies previously using Velocimo. Reps should identify eligible customers and have them complete the eligibility survey.

  • Technical & Contractual Requirements

    Agencies must switch to WorldPay as merchant of record and sign a sub-merchant agreement. PayPal is not supported. Set expectations with customers early.

  • Financial Impact

    Sample calculation for Fresno demonstrated how Secure Pay generates revenue based on transaction volume. Use the calculator to qualify — not every agency will pencil out.

  • Roadmap & GA Timeline

    General availability planned for fall. Upcoming: back office shopping cart, multi-merchant IDs, vanity URLs, self-service onboarding, refunds, and device support.
